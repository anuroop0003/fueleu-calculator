What this workbench does

This tool models compliance under the FuelEU Maritime regulation (EU 2023/1805): pooling vessel compliance balances, comparing allocation strategies, drafting a pooling agreement, banking/borrowing balances across years, estimating penalty exposure, and comparing fuel pathways against the regulatory GHG intensity target. All calculations run locally in your browser — nothing is sent anywhere.

Navigation & branding

Back / Home buttons
The back arrow returns you to the previous page in your browser; the home icon takes you to the home page.
Help button (this panel)
Opens this guide. Use the quick links above to jump to a section, or scroll through.

Tab 1 — Pooling

The core workspace for combining several vessels into one compliance pool.

Fleet register table
Enter each vessel's name and its adjusted compliance balance (tCO₂eq) — positive is surplus, negative is deficit. Add rows manually, load a sample fleet, import from CSV, or clear all rows.
KPI strip
Shows the pool's total balance, total surplus available, total deficit to cover, number of vessels, and overall pool status (compliant / non-compliant) at a glance.
Allocation method
Surplus from donor vessels (balance ≥ 0) is applied to deficit vessels until each reaches zero or the surplus runs out — donors with the smallest surplus are drawn first, and no donor is reduced below zero. The pool is compliant overall only if the total balance is non-negative.
Initial vs. allocated result panels
Two side-by-side cards show the fleet's balances before and after the allocation is applied, so you can see exactly how surplus moved between vessels.
Import & export tools
Import CSV, Export CSV, Print, and Export PDF let you bring in fleet data or take results out for filing/reporting.

Tab 2 — Scenario comparison

Click "Generate all scenarios" to automatically build four cards from your current fleet (entered on the Pooling tab), each applying a different internal transfer algorithm for distributing surplus to deficit vessels. Compare the four side by side to choose the allocation approach that best matches your pool's commercial agreement, then use "Clear all scenarios" to start over.

Tab 3 — Pool agreement

Generates a formal draft allocation record from your current Pooling tab results — a working draft to support the FuelEU database pooling declaration (due by 30 April).

Agreement metadata fields
Pool reference/name, compliance period, pool manager/lead company, and date of agreement — fill these in to populate the generated document.
Print / Export PDF
Produces a printable version of the agreement document for circulation and signature.

This is a drafting aid only — always have your verifier and legal counsel review the final agreement before submission.

Tab 4 — Multi-year banking

Tracks a single pool's compliance balance across multiple years, applying the regulation's banking and borrowing rules.

Banking
A surplus at the end of a period carries forward automatically to offset a future deficit.
Borrowing
A deficit can be partly covered by borrowing up to 2% of that period's required target × energy used — but not in two consecutive periods.
Ledger table columns
Period, energy used (MJ), balance before banking/borrowing, borrowed in, banked from prior, net after banking/borrowing, and carried forward — each row is one compliance year.
Toolbar
Add year, load a sample timeline, or clear all rows.
Summary KPIs
Final carried balance entering the next open period, number of years left with an uncovered deficit, and the maximum share of the 2% statutory borrowing ceiling used in any single year.

Tab 5 — Penalty calculator

Converts a residual compliance deficit (after pooling/banking/borrowing) into an estimated FuelEU penalty.

Pull residual deficit from Pooling tab
One click imports the current uncovered deficit from the Pooling tab, instead of typing it manually.
Input fields
Residual pool deficit (tCO₂eq), WtW GHG intensity (gCO₂eq/MJ), number of consecutive non-compliant years, VLSFO energy density (MJ/tonne), and the penalty rate (€/tonne VLSFO-eq) — all editable.
Statutory formula
The deficit is converted to tonnes of VLSFO-equivalent energy and charged at the rate entered (default €2,400/tonne, with 1 tonne VLSFO = 41,000 MJ default). The penalty escalates by 10 percentage points for each consecutive non-compliant year (110%, 120%, 130%…).
Result KPIs
Residual deficit, VLSFO-equivalent tonnage, the escalation factor applied, and the estimated total penalty payable — with a step-by-step breakdown beneath.

This is an indicative estimate only — your verifier's THETIS-MRV figures are authoritative for actual filing.

Tab 6 — Fuel comparison

Compares multiple fuel pathways' well-to-wake (WtW) GHG intensity against the FuelEU compliance target for a chosen reporting year.

Parameter bar
Reporting year (2025–2050, each with its own target intensity and required reduction %), annual energy demand (MJ), VLSFO energy density (MJ/t), and the penalty rate (€/t VLSFO-eq).
Fuel pathway table
For each fuel/pathway: WtW intensity, gap vs. target, compliance balance (tCO₂eq), non-compliant energy and VLSFO-equivalent tonnes (shown only where there's a deficit), and the estimated penalty or surplus value. Add a custom fuel row or reset the table to its defaults at any time.
GHG intensity chart
A bar chart of each fuel's WtW intensity against a dashed line marking the year's target — bars to the left of the line are compliant (surplus), bars to the right are non-compliant (deficit).

WtW intensities shown by default are common industry reference values (fossil per FuelEU Annex II; biofuel/RFNBO values vary by feedstock and certified Proof of Sustainability) — edit any cell to match your verifier-approved figures.

Important notes

All calculations are performed locally in your browser and are intended for planning and scenario exploration. They are not a substitute for verification by your accredited verifier, and should be checked against final figures reported through THETIS-MRV / the FuelEU database before any regulatory submission.
