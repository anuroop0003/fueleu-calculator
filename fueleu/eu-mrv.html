EU MRV & FuelEU Maritime — Voyage Classification Guide

This tool classifies vessel voyages under the EU MRV Regulation and FuelEU Maritime Regulation, working out how much of each voyage's emissions and fuel use falls inside EU scope. Bring in voyage data, let the tool classify each leg against the port registry, then review and export the results.

How Each Voyage Type Is Scoped — Diagram

Every voyage leg is classified by comparing the departure port and arrival port against the Port Registry. The combination of the two determines what share of that leg's CO₂ and fuel consumption is counted under EU MRV / FuelEU Maritime. The six diagrams below show exactly how a vessel moving between ports is treated in each case.

① EEA → EEA 100% Scope

Both the departure and arrival ports sit inside the EEA. The entire distance sailed, fuel burned and CO₂ emitted on this leg is 100% in scope for both EU MRV and FuelEU Maritime — nothing is apportioned out.

② EEA → Non‑EEA 50% Scope

Departs an EEA port and arrives outside the EEA. Because only one end of the leg is in Union waters, EU rules count 50% of the CO₂ & fuel for this leg toward MRV / FuelEU compliance — the "outside" portion is excluded.

③ Non‑EEA → EEA 50% Scope

Departs a non‑EEA port and arrives at an EEA port — the mirror image of Case ②. Again 50% of the leg's CO₂ & fuel counts toward EU obligations, reflecting the portion of the voyage that is inbound to the Union.

④ EEA Port Call (In‑Port) 100% In‑Port

Whenever a vessel is within an EEA port — berthed, maneuvering or at anchorage — that time is captured at 100% scope under both regulations, regardless of how the connecting sea passage on either side is classified.

⑤ OMR Voyage Tracked Separately

Either port is an Outermost Region (e.g. Canary Islands, Azores, Madeira, French overseas departments). These routes carry their own transitional/derogated treatment under EU rules, so the tool flags them as OMR and reports them separately rather than folding them into the standard 100% / 50% buckets.

⑥ Out of Scope 0% Excluded

Departure and arrival are both outside the EEA (and neither is an OMR port). The leg has no MRV / FuelEU obligation at all and is excluded entirely from CO₂ and compliance totals — it still appears in the results table for completeness, tagged "Out of Scope".

The MRV and FuelEU classification for a leg can diverge — e.g. a port might be treated as in-scope under one regulation but not the other. Where that happens, the Results tab flags the row with the ⚠ MRV ≠ FuelEU filter so it can be reviewed manually.

OMR — Outward Most Remote Voyages
⚠️ Under EU MRV Regulation, voyages to/from Outermost Regions (OMR) of the EU have special classification rules. OMR ports are treated as Non-EEA for scope determination.

EU Outermost Regions (Article 349 TFEU): Guadeloupe, French Guiana, Martinique, Réunion, Mayotte, Saint-Martin (France), Azores, Madeira (Portugal), Canary Islands (Spain). The exact list of ports treated as EEA, Non-EEA, or OMR is maintained in the editable Port Classification Registry on the 📖 Regulation Guide tab — update it there if a port's category changes.

EU MRV Regulation (EU) 2015/757 — Scope Rules

The EU MRV Regulation applies to ships >5,000 GT calling at EU/EEA ports. CO₂ emissions are monitored based on voyage type:

EEA → EEA (100%)

Both departure and arrival ports are within EEA. 100% of fuel and CO₂ emissions reported.

EEA → Non-EEA (50%)

Departure is EEA port, arrival is outside EEA. 50% of emissions counted toward MRV obligation.

Non-EEA → EEA (50%)

Departure is outside EEA, arrival is EEA port. 50% of emissions counted toward MRV obligation.

EEA Port Call (100%)

Emissions during stay at EEA port (anchorage, maneuvering). 100% in-scope.

Non-EEA → Non-EEA voyages with no EEA port call are out of scope for EU MRV.

FuelEU Maritime (EU) 2023/1805 — Scope Rules

FuelEU Maritime applies from 1 January 2025 to ships >5,000 GT. It sets GHG intensity targets for energy used:

  • EEA→EEA: 100% of energy used on voyage is within scope
  • EEA→Non-EEA / Non-EEA→EEA: 50% of energy used is within scope
  • At EEA port ≥2 hours: All energy used at berth is in scope
  • OMR voyages: Treated as Non-EEA for FuelEU scope

Energy is calculated as Lower Calorific Value (LCV). Default: HFO = 40.2 MJ/kg, VLSFO = 40.5 MJ/kg, LNG = 48.0 MJ/kg, MDO/MGO = 42.7 MJ/kg.

GHG Intensity Reduction Targets

2025: −2% | 2030: −6% | 2035: −14.5% | 2040: −31% | 2045: −62% | 2050: −80% (vs 2020 baseline of 91.16 gCO₂eq/MJ)
