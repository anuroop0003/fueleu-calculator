CF 905 — Biofuel Carbon Factor Calculator
How to use this page — inputs, calculated fields & toolbar functions
This calculator computes the Carbon Correction Factor (CF) for a biofuel blend per IMO regulation MEPC.354(78) / MARPOL Annex VI. Enter the fuel quantities and biofuel properties in Part A, the auto-calculated fraction breakdown appears in Part B, and the final CF value and calculated CO₂ emissions are shown in Part C.
Toolbar Buttons
Calculate Now Validates all Part A inputs and runs the CF calculation. Part B fractions and Part C results are updated instantly. Required fields are highlighted if incomplete.
Save XML Exports all current input values as a structured XML file to your device. Use to archive a session or share with colleagues for re-import later.
Import XML Restores all input values from a previously saved XML file, including any uploaded Excel blend data. All fields are populated exactly as they were when saved.
Print / PDF Generates a formatted A4 print report containing all inputs, the Part B fraction breakdown, and the Part C CF result and CO₂ emissions. Use browser print-to-PDF to save.
Reset Clears all input fields, removes any uploaded Excel data, and hides Part C results. Part B returns to dashes. Use to start a fresh calculation from scratch.
Part A — Inputs
All fields marked ^ are required. Fields marked # are required if the corresponding ^ field is left blank. Enter values manually or upload a multi-blend Excel file.
Total fuel quantity loaded (MT) Total mass of the blended fuel loaded onto the vessel, in metric tonnes. This is the combined mass of biofuel fraction plus fossil fuel fraction. Source from the Bunker Delivery Note (BDN).
Quantity of biofuel fraction ^ (MT) Mass of the biofuel component only, in metric tonnes. If you have the mass directly from the BDN, enter it here. If you only have volume, leave this blank and provide m³ and density instead.
Quantity of biofuel fraction ^ (m³) Volume of the biofuel component in cubic metres. Used together with density (kg/m³) to derive mass when MT is not available. Leave blank if MT is provided.
Density of biofuel fraction # (kg/m³) Density of the biofuel at 15°C in kg/m³, from the biofuel certificate or PoS document. Required only when the biofuel quantity is provided in m³ rather than MT.
Total Energy from biofuel fraction ^ (MJ) Total energy content of the biofuel fraction in megajoules, calculated as mass (kg) × Lower Calorific Value (MJ/kg). This value is taken from the Proof of Sustainability (PoS) certificate for the biofuel.
Emission intensity of biofuel ^ (g CO₂e/MJ) The well-to-wake greenhouse gas emission intensity of the biofuel, in grams CO₂-equivalent per megajoule. Taken directly from the PoS or sustainability certificate. For fossil-like biofuels with intensity above 33 g CO₂e/MJ, the calculator applies the distillate fuel CF of 3.206.
Fuel grade of fossil fraction # Select the grade of the fossil fuel component from the drop-down. Each grade has fixed CF and LCV values per MEPC.354(78): Distillate = CF 3.206, LCV 42.7 MJ/kg · Residual RMA–RMD = CF 3.151, LCV 41.2 MJ/kg · Residual RME–RMK = CF 3.114, LCV 40.2 MJ/kg.
Excel Template / Upload / Clear Excel Template downloads a pre-formatted .xlsx with the correct column headers. Fill in one row per blend. Upload Excel imports the completed file and displays all blend rows in an editable table — rows can be added or removed individually. Clear removes all uploaded data and restores the manual input form.
Part B — Biofuel & Fossil Fraction Breakdown (Auto-calculated)
No input required. All values update automatically as you type in Part A.
Biofuel Weight (% mass) Biofuel mass as a percentage of total fuel loaded. Calculated as: (Biofuel kg ÷ Total fuel kg) × 100.
Biofuel Weight (kg) Biofuel mass in kilograms. Derived from the MT input, or from m³ × density if MT was not provided.
LCV of biofuel (MJ/kg) Lower Calorific Value of the biofuel fraction, derived as: Total biofuel energy (MJ) ÷ Biofuel mass (kg). This is used to calculate the biofuel CF.
CF of biofuel (g‑CO₂/g fuel) Carbon correction factor for the biofuel fraction. Calculated as: (LCV ÷ 1000) × Emission intensity. If emission intensity exceeds 33 g CO₂e/MJ, the distillate CF value of 3.206 is applied instead.
Energy content of biofuel (MJ) Passed through directly from the Part A input — the total energy of the biofuel fraction in megajoules.
Fossil fraction weight (% & kg) Remainder of the total fuel after subtracting the biofuel fraction. Fossil kg = Total kg − Biofuel kg. Fossil % = 100 − Biofuel %.
CF of fossil fuel (g‑CO₂/g fuel) Fixed CF value for the selected fossil fuel grade per MEPC.354(78). Distillate = 3.206 · Residual RMA–RMD = 3.151 · Residual RME–RMK = 3.114.
Energy content of fossil fuel (MJ) Calculated as: Fossil fuel mass (kg) × LCV of the selected fossil fuel grade (MJ/kg).
Part C — Results (shown after Calculate Now)
CF value of the blend (g‑CO₂/g fuel) The final energy-weighted CF for the blended fuel. Formula: [(Biofuel energy × Biofuel CF) + (Fossil energy × Fossil CF)] ÷ Total energy. This is the value to be reported under MARPOL Annex VI for IMO DCS purposes.
Calculated CO₂ emissions (tonnes) Total CO₂ emissions for the fuel quantity. Calculated as: Blend CF × Total fuel mass (MT). When multiple blends are uploaded via Excel, a separate CO₂ figure is shown for each blend in the results table below.
Multiple biofuel blend results table Appears when an Excel file with multiple blend rows is uploaded. Displays per-blend: bio energy (MJ), fossil fuel grade, blend CF, and calculated CO₂ tonnes. Each row corresponds to one uploaded blend.
Step-by-Step Workflow
1 Obtain the BDN (Bunker Delivery Note) and the biofuel Proof of Sustainability (PoS) certificate for the delivery being assessed.
2 Enter total fuel quantity, biofuel quantity (MT or m³+density), biofuel energy (MJ), emission intensity (g CO₂e/MJ) and fossil fuel grade in Part A. For multiple blends, use the Excel template.
3 Review the auto-populated Part B fraction breakdown to verify the biofuel % mass, LCV and individual CF values are as expected.
4 Click Calculate Now. The final blend CF and CO₂ emissions appear in Part C. Use Print / PDF to produce the formal record, or Save XML to archive.
